This months focus

Marine plastic pollution

Marine plastic pollution refers to the accumulation of plastic objects and particles in the Earth's oceans and seas. This widespread issue not only disrupts shipping routes and naval operations but also poses significant challenges to maintaining clean and navigable waters globally.

COMPASS

The Facts

Plastic constitutes 80% of all marine debris.

Over 400 million tons of plastic are produced annually, and at least 14 million tons of this plastic end up in the ocean each year.

Plastic pollution threatens food safety and quality, human health, coastal tourism, and contributes to climate change.

Over 100,000 marine mammals are estimated to die each year due to marine plastic pollution.

Living Sustainably

The Checklist

  • Avoid single-use plastics like straws, bags, and water bottles
  • Participate in local beach or river cleanups
  • Choose products with minimal plastic packaging
  • Buy essential plastic packaged items in bulk to minimise average plastic use
  • Support and buy from companies committed to sustainable packaging
  • Properly dispose of and recycle plastic waste to prevent it from entering waterways.
  • Use a microplastic filter in washing machines to reduce synthetic fibre pollution
  • Choose natural fibre clothing over synthetic fabrics to minimise microplastic shedding
  • Educate others about the impact of plastic pollution on marine life and ecosystems
  • Advocate for policies and regulations that reduce plastic production
